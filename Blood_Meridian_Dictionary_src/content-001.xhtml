 chines

(noun) 1. Narrow ravines or gorges cutting through a ridge; steep-sided clefts in a hillside or ridgeline. Primarily used in English dialect (especially southern England) for coastal gorges, here applied to rocky desert ridges.

“running down the narrow chines of the ridges”

(noun) 2. Bare ridges or spines of rock projecting along a hillside or mountainside; the exposed backbone of a rocky formation.

“the naked chines of rock blown bare of snow”

 Chiricahua

(proper noun) A band of the Apache people whose territory spanned southeastern Arizona, southwestern New Mexico, and the adjacent mountains of northern Sonora and Chihuahua; among the most formidable raiders in the borderlands, with leaders including Cochise and Geronimo.

“small scattered bands of Chiricahua raiders”

 Chiricahuas

(proper noun) One of the main divisions of the Apache people, whose traditional territory covered southeastern Arizona, southwestern New Mexico, and adjacent northern Mexico. In 1849 they were the dominant hostile force along this stretch of the Santa Cruz corridor.

“The Chiricahuas”
