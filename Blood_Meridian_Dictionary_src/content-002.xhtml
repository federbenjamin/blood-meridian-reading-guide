 Doniphan

(person / historical) Colonel Alexander William Doniphan (1808–1887), Missouri lawyer and officer who commanded the First Missouri Mounted Volunteers during the Mexican–American War, leading them on a celebrated march through New Mexico and Chihuahua.

“Doniphan’s command pulled east for Saltillo”

 dorys

(noun) Variant spelling of “dories”; small, shallow, flat-bottomed boats with high flaring sides, traditionally used by fishermen. Here applied metaphorically to the hide-covered houses crouching along the beach in a row.

“houses were made of hides ranged like curious dorys along the selvage of sea oats”
