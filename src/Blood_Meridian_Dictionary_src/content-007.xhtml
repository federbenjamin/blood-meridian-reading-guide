 ricks

(noun) Large, neatly stacked outdoor piles of material (normally hay or straw); here enormous stacks of buffalo bones awaiting shipment.

“They passed enormous ricks of bones”

 ride and tie

(phrase) A method by which two people share one horse: one rides ahead a certain distance, ties the horse, then walks on; the other catches up to the tied horse, mounts, rides past the first person, and repeats the cycle.

“We could ride and tie.”

 rigged a broken lyre

(phrase) The silent lightning to the north illuminated the dark horizon in jagged bolts shaped like the strings of a lyre (a harp-like stringed instrument of antiquity), but broken and irregular.

“the silent lightning rigged a broken lyre upon the world’s dark rim”
