(noun) Los Hermanos Penitentes, a lay Catholic confraternity of Hispanic men in northern New Mexico and southern Colorado, known for austere Lenten self-flagellation processions and Passion reenactments.

“The penitent brothers”

 peons

(noun) 1. In the Mexican context, laborers bound to an estate by debt peonage — a system in which a small advance of money created a debt obligation, with interest manipulated so the debt could never be repaid, binding the worker in perpetual servitude.

“they looked like God’s profoundest peons”

(noun) 2. In 19th-century Mexico, landless agricultural laborers, often bound to an estate by debt peonage; lower-class rural workers with severely restricted freedom of movement.

“The peons in the fields saw them”

 Perdida

(Spanish) “Lost” (feminine singular); here repeated as a lament that the card — and by extension the fortune — has been lost in the night.

“Perdida, perdida. La carta esta perdida en la noche.”
